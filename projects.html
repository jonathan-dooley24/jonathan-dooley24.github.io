Projects Overview

A brief look at 3 of my favorite projects that I've worked on in the past - through school and other work.

Misty II Skills Programming

The Misty II robot is a highly-versatile robot that is created by Misty Robotics. Her applications and capabilities are incredibly wide-ranging - and that is by design! She is used in hospitals, schools, retail, libraries, and more.

I was fortunate to get to work a Misty robot during my time with a start-up company called Useabot. Part of my role on the project was to research and gain an understanding of the Misty SDK that developers use to program skills Misty can perform. I enjoyed learning all about Misty and was even able to meet Ian Bernstein, an accomplished roboticist, entrepeneuer and Founder/CEO of Misty Robotics.

The Misty skill I helped develop was used in Useabot showcases of the Misty II robot and has eventually been loaded into robots that are now used to entertain and help children in a children's hostpital in Minnesota.

Pipelined CPU Simulator

Computer Architecture is a class I took at UST during my Junior year. It was focused largely on understanding instruction sets, processors, and memory. One of our assignments, known notorisouly to some students, was to build a simulated processor that implements pipelining - a technicque where instructions are carried out in simpler steps concurrently.

My teammate and I built our simulated processor in C. We recycled a simulated compiler from a previous project and used our simulated CPU to turn simple instruction sets into machine-readable code and executed the instructions to do basic tasks like arithmetic, loops, and function calls.

Phong Shading 3D Graphics

Computer Graphics is a class I took at UST during my Sophomore year. It was focused on 2D and 3D rendering and how computers can turn code into a visual representation of objects. One of my favorite projects was focused on shading algorithms and how the presence of a light source should affect the shading/illumination of objects in a 3D scene.

I used the (relatively) famous Phong Algorithm and OpenGL, an open-source rendering software, to make a 3D scene with shading. It was very cool to see this and gave me an extra appreciation for the CGI we see in movies and video games, today.
